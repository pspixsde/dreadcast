## Setting

The world resembles **medieval times** in broad strokes, but sits on its **own map** with its own geography and history.

**Life and nature** — Many creatures, races, animals, and plants populate it. Some are infused with magic; others are magical in themselves.

**Politics** — Multiple **dynasties and countries** vie for power. They are violent and hard to survive under; existence is rarely comfortable.

**Magic** — Magic is **common knowledge** but **not common practice**. Only certain people can wield it: those **born with the gift**, or those who rely on **magical items** and similar sources. (Acquisition through other means may exist; most folk never see the full picture.)

**The “pure”** — People with any **innate** tie to magic are called **pure**. They are treated as a **distinct people** in law, rumor, and custom, even though they are **not** morphologically or anatomically different from anyone else.

**Cursed places** — There are **cursed regions**: some hoard magical relics, some radiate a cursed aura, some are intrinsically magical from within. The varieties multiply; commoners lump them together as “bad ground.”

**What people think magic is** — Layfolk mostly know **two** names: **pure magic** (associated with the born-talented) and **dark magic** (whispers, fear, almost no reliable detail).

**Practitioners** — The world knows **sorcerers, warlocks, mages, druids**, and similar paths. There are **no** paladin orders, no widely trusted “pure-hearted” institutions, and no castes defined by virtue. **Everyone with power is at least self-interested**; altruism, when it appears, is personal, not systemic.

**Tone** — The world is **harsh and unforgiving**. **Life expectancy is low.**

## The Dread Pit

**The Dread Pit** is this setting’s answer to **hell or purgatory**: a prison of suffering and broken minds.

When **mortals** arrive there, they are **fated** to have their minds **eroded**—to **lose what was human and sensible** in them—and to be **tormented** in ways **imps** (and other dwellers) invent or relish.

The Pit has **many dwellers** and is said to contain **nine circles** (structure and geography to be expanded later).

## Characters

All **playable characters** begin in **the Dread Pit**, each for their own reason. Their stories converge where the game starts.

### Undead Hunter

**Summary** — A hunter who lived by honest work and fair dealing, cast out for **rumors of dark magic in his blood**. He built a quiet life at the forest’s edge until a **cursed beast** and **human cruelty** destroyed everything he loved. He died with a vow of vengeance—and woke in the Pit, **half-dead**, **dulled**, and **hungry** in ways that no longer feel entirely human.

**Backstory**

He was a **hard-working hunter**: tough but fair, providing for his family. **Local rumor** painted him as heir to **dark magic**; his people were **ostracized**. He **moved deep into remote woods** and built a **house with his own hands**, hoping distance would keep his **wife and son** safe.

For a time, life was **quiet**.

One hunt, he tracked a **huge black boar**. It would feed them for a long while. He **lined up his best shot** and struck it **square in the head**. The beast **only staggered**. Half its face was **torn away**; the **remaining eye** burned **red**; its **tusks lengthened and sharpened**; a **dark glow** clung to it. **Magic**, he knew—and he **turned to run**. **Too late.** The boar **gored** him. He **fought** until strength failed; his sight **narrowed**; he believed he was **dying**.

He **woke** in **cold sweat**, skull **splitting**, **voices** pressing from every direction. **Night**. **Wounded**, he **crawled** toward home, **weakening** with every length of ground, **clinging** to the thought of his family.

When he reached the house, **dread** struck before he crossed the threshold. The **door** was **ripped** open; the **rooms** **trashed**; **glass** **shattered**. **Not an animal**—**people**. He **staggered inside** anyway.

By the **bedroom** he saw a **foot** and **dared to hope**. **Hope ended there.** He found **his wife and his son** **dead**. He was **sure** he would **follow**—but in that moment he **swore**: **whatever it took**, **however long**, he would **avenge** them.

He **collapsed** over their bodies and **died**.

**After** — His next awareness is **the Dread Pit**. He is **changed**: clothes **reduced to ash**, skin **half-dead**, **feelings** **muted**. Little remains in his mind but **bloodlust** or **emptiness**; almost nothing feels **human** as it once did.

**From here, the game begins.**
